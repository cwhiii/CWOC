What Are Omni Chits?

An Omni Chit is a single, flexible record that adapts to whatever you need it to be — a task, a note, a calendar event, an alarm, a checklist, or an entire project. Instead of scattering your life across five different apps, everything lives in one place under one unified data model.

One Chit, Many Roles

A chit becomes what you put into it. Add a date and it's a calendar event. Add checklist items and it's a checklist. Flag it with a status and it's a task. Write markdown content with no date and it's a note. Attach child chits and it becomes a project with its own Kanban board. Set an alert and it's an alarm. A single chit can be all of these at once — or just one. You decide.

Six Views, One Collection

Your chits are automatically organized into six views — collectively called C CAPTN:

  • Calendar — chits with dates and times, shown across week, day, month, year, itinerary, and X-day views
  • Checklists — chits with nested, drag-and-drop checklist items
  • Alarms — chits with alarm or notification flags
  • Projects — master chits with child chits arranged in Kanban-style boards
  • Tasks — chits with a status: To Do, In Progress, Blocked, or Complete
  • Notes — markdown-powered chits with no dates attached

You never have to categorize anything manually. The system reads what's in the chit and puts it where it belongs — often in more than one view at the same time.

Why It's Useful

Real life doesn't fit neatly into "task" or "event" or "note." A doctor's appointment is a calendar event, a checklist of questions to ask, and a task to schedule a follow-up — all at once. Omni Chits let you capture that naturally without duplicating information or jumping between apps. Everything about a thing stays together in one record, visible from every angle that matters.

How It's Different

Most productivity tools force you to choose upfront: is this a task or a note? A calendar event or a reminder? Then they lock you into that choice. Omni Chits don't. There's no rigid type system, no separate databases for separate concepts, and no syncing between disconnected modules.

There's also no account to create, no subscription to manage, and no cloud dependency. CWOC is self-hosted, private, and yours. Your data sits in a single SQLite file on hardware you control — not on someone else's server behind a paywall. Nothing is ever hard-deleted; soft delete means you can always recover what you thought you didn't need.

It's a small, fast, no-framework web app with a 1940s parchment aesthetic — because productivity tools don't have to look like spreadsheets.
